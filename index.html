Programme outline

Scientific content & timetable

Three progressive programmes totalling 12 sessions and ~14.5 hours of structured learning. Each session is designed as a standalone 75–90 minute unit suitable for facilitated group delivery or self-directed study.

Part 1
AI-Ready Clinician
3 sessions  ·  ~3.5 hours total  ·  Target: clinicians with uncertain or no AI knowledge
Session Title Scientific content Duration
S1 AI is Already Here.
Where Are You?		 AI fundamentals: definitions, ML vs DL vs LLM vs AGI. AI hierarchy and augmented intelligence. Current AI applications in clinical practice: radiology, pathology, NLP, ambient scribes. Personal AI self-assessment (4-stage framework). Facilitator narrative: My Story. No-device session — discussion and reflection only. 60–90 min
S2 Let's Actually
Use It		 Hands-on with general-purpose LLMs (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini). Prompt engineering principles. Clinical AI tools in practice: AI scribes, radiology AI, diagnostic decision support. The 5 Questions framework for evaluating any AI tool. Common failure modes: hallucination, bias, overconfidence. Live demonstrations and participant exercises with clinical scenarios. 75 min
S3 AI, Ethics &
My Responsibility		 AI ethics principles: WHO framework, EU trustworthiness standards. MMC Guideline on Ethical Use of AI in Medical Practice (2025). Accountability, informed consent, and clinical override. Three case-based ethics scenarios (Scenarios A, B, C) with group discussion. Administrator segment: governance and institutional obligations. Reflection quiz and completion form. 75 min
Part 1 Total  ·  Supplementary: AI, ML, AI agents, API & MCP concepts guide with infographic ~3.5 hrs
Part 2
AI-Steady Clinician
4 sessions  ·  ~5 hours total  ·  Target: AI-familiar clinicians  ·  Based on Chew & Ngiam, BMC Medicine 2025
Session Title Scientific content Duration
S1 Why AI? Problems, Teams & Levels of Use The 9-step AI tool development cycle (Chew & Ngiam 2025). Three levels of AI usage: general use, fine-tuning, new model from scratch. Identifying clinical problems suitable for AI (descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, prescriptive). Signs, symptoms, and actions framework. Ideal team composition (12 roles). Crossing the chasm: six barriers to personalised AI in healthcare. Programmed systems vs true AI tools. 75 min
S2 Data, Infrastructure & Building the Model Data quality, representativeness, interoperability, and de-identification. Supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement, transfer, and federated learning. Data annotation and inter-rater reliability — the clinician's irreplaceable role. AI system pipeline: input, processing, output stages. Infrastructure: cloud vs on-premise vs federated. Information leakage and its prevention. Class imbalance handling. 75 min
S3 Validating, Evaluating & Registering an AI Tool Validation ladder: internal, external, prospective, sandboxing. Key performance metrics: sensitivity, specificity, AUC, calibration, net benefit. Subgroup performance and fairness analysis. Explainability tools: SHAP, LIME, Grad-CAM. Reporting frameworks: TRIPOD+AI, CONSORT-AI, SPIRIT-AI, DECIDE-AI, MI-CLAIM, APPRAISE-AI, OPTICA. FDA regulatory purview and exclusions. MMC 2025 guideline. Malaysian regulatory context. 75 min
S4 Deployment, Monitoring & the AI Ecosystem Deployment strategies: copilot, asynchronous, autonomous. Alert fatigue: causes, consequences, prevention. Integration with clinical workflows and EMR systems. Post-deployment model drift detection. The 8-component AI ecosystem. Four challenges in healthcare AI: team, infrastructure, awareness, policy. Accountability structures and governance escalation. Prof Kee Yuan Ngiam (NUHS): Implementing AI in Practice (video). Reflection quiz and completion form. 75 min
Part 2 Total  ·  Supplementary: 65-term interactive AI/ML glossary ~5 hrs
Part 3
AI-Savvy Clinician
5 sessions  ·  ~6 hours total  ·  Target: clinicians ready to design AI projects  ·  Based on ETEPAI (Chew & Ngiam, JMIR AI 2026)
Session Title Scientific content Duration
S1 The ETEPAI Framework & Readiness Probes ETEPAI overview: synthesising 30 frameworks into 4 stages × 3 domains. The proliferation problem in AI guidelines. Three domains: ethics, technical, epidemiological. Four stages: design, develop, deploy, post-deployment. Probing questions for each stage (28 questions total). Why clinicians are irreplaceable in the epidemiological domain. EU trustworthiness standards and ETEPAI alignment. 75 min
S2 Design Stage — Ethics, Technical & Epidemiological Ethics domain: beneficence, fairness by design, transparency, data rights, stakeholder engagement, conflict of interest. Technical domain: problem framing, algorithm selection rationale, data availability audit, infrastructure planning, team composition. Epidemiological domain: PICO framework application, study design selection, outcome definition, sample size (EPV rule), selection bias. Design stage as the most consequential and most neglected stage. 75 min
S3 Development Stage — Building with Rigour Ethics: bias detection protocols, audit trails, transparency commitments, IP and data sharing agreements. Technical: data preprocessing standards, train/validation/test split, information leakage prevention, model selection rationale, internal validation, code documentation and version control. Epidemiological: labelling strategy, inter-rater reliability (kappa), class imbalance correction, confounding management, pre-specified performance metrics (AUC, calibration, sensitivity/specificity). External validation as mandatory gate. 75 min
S4 Deploy & Post-Deployment — Real-World Integration Ethics: informed consent for AI-assisted decisions, equitable access, mandatory override mechanisms, alert design ethics. Technical: sandboxing protocol, EMR integration architecture, rollback procedures, staff training requirements. Epidemiological: prospective external validation, decision curve analysis, health technology assessment, real-world evidence study design, post-market surveillance (model drift). Accountability structures and governance pre-deployment. MMC 2025 guideline obligations. 75 min
S5 Pitfalls, Team Building & Capstone Project Seven common pitfalls with prevention strategies: solving the wrong problem, information leakage, biased training data, absent human comparator, internal validation only, no real-world impact evidence, absent post-deployment governance. Interactive team-builder: 12 project roles, essential vs required vs optional. AI Research Proposal Template: 17 sections (A–O) including topic, literature review, conceptual framework, PICO, study design, methods, sampling, data analysis plan, discussion (shortfalls, limitations, and their distinctions), ethics, budget, Gantt chart, supplementary materials, AI disclosure, and do's and don'ts of AI-assisted writing. Companion interactive web tools for clinical researchers (quantitative and qualitative proposal builders, data analysis studios, academic writing and publication guides). Capstone reflection quiz. Completion form. 90 min
Part 3 Total  ·  Supplementary: 17-section AI Research Proposal Template (Multimedia Appendix 3, ETEPAI)  ·  Companion interactive research tools at ai-clinical-research.netlify.app ~6 hrs
